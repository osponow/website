14 November 2025

OSPO Now joins the OpenChain Partner network

by Dr Raphael Sonabend-Friend

Were thrilled to share that OSPO Now has been accepted as an official OpenChain partner. This milestone recognises our teams track record helping organisations build, manage, and mature secure and compliant open source projects with confidence.

Why this matters

OpenChain is the Linux Foundation project behind two international standards that make open source use safer and more predictable across the software supply chain.

  • ISO/IEC 5230: the international standard for open source licence compliance.
  • ISO/IEC 18974: the international standard for open source security assurance.

These standards provide a clear and lightweight framework to reduce risk and increase compliance when building and using open source products.

What our partnership unlocks

As an OpenChain partner, OSPO Now is recognised as a service provider qualified to support small and large organisations to safely adopt open source. This partnership affirms our belief that a strong OSPO, whether in-house or delivered as a service, is pivotal to scalable compliance and sustainable open source adoption.

"An extensive community of service providers is key to enabling choice across the market. One reason we are delighted to work with OSPO Now is to enable such choice. Another is that the emergence of OSPOs has provided a pivotal opportunity for companies to improve their process management around open source, and direct expertise in this domain is a vital part of ensuring health."

Shane Coughlan | OpenChain, General Manager

How we help

OSPO Now's mission is to empower organisations through the strategic use of open source solutions. One of our core services is to support organisations meet the highest security and compliance standards, our partnership with OpenChain signifies that we have the right expertise to help you on this journey. By providing expert consulting, and access to our proprietary Codort tool, we can help you self-certify as meeting both the ISO/IEC 5230 and ISO/IEC 18974.

"This recognition underscores our commitment to helping organisations build, manage, and mature their open source programmes with confidence. Partnering with OpenChain validates the expertise of our team in guiding organisations along their open source compliance journey."

Raphael Sonabend-Friend | OSPO Now, CEO
